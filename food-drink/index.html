Our story & sourcing

One fire, and the patience to let it speak.

Head Chef Elena Vasquez built Ember & Olive around a conviction she has held since cooking over her grandmother's coals as a child: that the finest ingredient, handled with restraint, needs almost nothing done to it. There is no gas line in her kitchen. Everything passes through a single wood-fired hearth, where vine cuttings and fallen ironbark lend their perfume to every plate. Her philosophy is one of subtraction — reading an ingredient at its peak, then stepping back so its character, and the quiet transformation of the flame, carry the dish.

That restraint begins long before service. Elena and her team keep standing relationships with a small circle of growers, fishers and foragers within a day's drive of the room — the biodynamic market garden that reserves its first pick for us, the diver who lands scallops by hand, the single orchard whose heritage quince we roast each autumn. Produce is chosen at dawn and cooked by dusk; menus are written around what the season is willing to give, never the other way around. What cannot be sourced with conscience is simply left off the plate. It is farm-to-table not as a slogan, but as the only way she has ever known how to cook.
